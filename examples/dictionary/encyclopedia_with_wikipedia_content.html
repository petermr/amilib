Definition

Climate change adaptation refers to the process of adjusting to actual or expected climate and its effects. It involves taking practical actions to manage risks from climate impacts, protect communities, and strengthen the resilience of the economy.

Source: Wikipedia - Climate Change Adaptation

Description

Adaptation strategies can be implemented at various levels, from individual actions to national policies. Examples include building sea walls to protect against rising sea levels, developing drought-resistant crops, improving water management systems, and creating early warning systems for extreme weather events. Successful adaptation requires understanding local climate risks, engaging stakeholders, and implementing flexible solutions that can evolve as conditions change.

Definition

Carbon dioxide (chemical formula CO₂) is a colorless, odorless gas that is vital to life on Earth. It is a naturally occurring chemical compound composed of one carbon atom covalently double bonded to two oxygen atoms.

Source: Wikipedia - Carbon Dioxide

Description

Carbon dioxide is a crucial component of the carbon cycle, where it is exchanged between the atmosphere, oceans, soil, and living organisms. Plants use CO₂ during photosynthesis to produce oxygen and organic compounds, while animals and other organisms release CO₂ through respiration and decomposition. Human activities, particularly the burning of fossil fuels, have significantly increased atmospheric CO₂ concentrations, contributing to climate change.

Definition

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global weather patterns and average temperatures. While climate change has occurred throughout Earth's history, the current period of warming is occurring more rapidly than any past events and is primarily driven by human activities.

Source: Wikipedia - Climate Change

Description

The primary driver of current climate change is the increase in greenhouse gas concentrations in the atmosphere, particularly carbon dioxide from burning fossil fuels. This leads to global warming, which causes widespread changes including rising sea levels, more frequent extreme weather events, shifts in precipitation patterns, and impacts on ecosystems and human societies. Addressing climate change requires both mitigation (reducing emissions) and adaptation (preparing for impacts).

Source: Wikipedia - Climate Change

Definition

Greenhouse gases are gases in Earth's atmosphere that trap heat by absorbing infrared radiation. They allow sunlight to pass through the atmosphere but prevent the heat from escaping back into space, creating a natural greenhouse effect that keeps Earth warm enough to support life.

Source: Wikipedia - Greenhouse Gas

Description

These gases include carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), nitrous oxide (N₂O), and fluorinated gases. They contribute to the greenhouse effect, which is essential for maintaining Earth's temperature but can lead to global warming when concentrations increase due to human activities such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes.
